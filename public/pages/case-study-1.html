CASE STUDY

1. Introduction:

  • Provide a brief overview of the design project, including the problem or challenge addressed, the stakeholders involved, and the overall goals.
  • Introduce the context of the project and set the stage for the reader to understand the subsequent sections.

2. Project Background:

  • Offer more detailed information about the background and context of the project.
  • Discuss any relevant industry trends, market conditions, or user behaviors that influenced the project. Provide a clear understanding of why the project was initiated.

3. Problem Statement:

  • Clearly define the problem or challenge that the design project aimed to solve.
  • Articulate the problem statement in a concise and focused manner. This section helps readers understand the specific issues that needed addressing.

4. User Research:

  • Share insights gained from the research conducted to understand the target users and their needs.
  • Include information about the research methods employed, key findings, user personas, and any other relevant data that informed the design process.

5. Ideation and Brainstorming

  • Describe the ideation phase where potential solutions were generated
  • Discuss brainstorming sessions, workshops, or collaborative activities used to generate a variety of ideas. Highlight key concepts and how they were initially evaluated.

6. Prototyping:

  • Explain how design concepts were translated into tangible prototypes.
  • Showcase different iterations of prototypes, from low-fidelity to high-fidelity versions. Discuss the feedback received during prototyping and how it influenced subsequent iterations.
