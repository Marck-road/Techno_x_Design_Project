CASE STUDY

1. Introduction:

Nowadays, technology has also become prominent due to society witnessing its advancements by making everyday life gradually easier and more convenient. Thus, there is a constant need for better industry, innovation, and infrastructure to develop new technologies and keep up with the modern world to meet the demands of the people in a sustainable manner.

Queueing has always been an integral part of society to keep everyone in the society more organized in handling their transactions. However, it is common to see long queue lines, especially in businesses, payment establishments, and hospitals. People would sometimes have to wait long hours on the site for their turn. Time is a precious resource that no one wants to waste.

This case study tackles how businesses and establishments can upgrade and innovate their queueing systems and transactions and give the general public a much more efficient and hassle-free queueing experience.

2. Project Background:

Queuebytes aims to change how queueing works in the Philippines by providing a virtual queueing system that eliminates the long wait in physical lines. Queuebytes seeks to revolutionize the queueing experience by having both the users and businesses benefit from the system.

Traditional queueing methods lead to longer wait times, frustration, and wasted time for the customer and the business. Which could then lead to customer dissatisfaction, feeling of inconvenience and a loss of productivity for the customer and business side of things. This queueing system is outdated and inefficient. Queuebytes was initiated to address these drawbacks and create a more modern and user-friendly queueing solution.

3. Problem Statement:

Queuing has always been a struggle especially in public establishments. With, traditional queuing system in businesses and establishments it could cause disorganization that causes wasting time. This inefficiency is due to:

  • Manual or Traditional Process - relies on paper-based ticketing system which reduces the efficiency of the staffs
  • Lack of staffs - staffs are not readily available in handling large volumes of customers
  • Limited queue management - establishments struggles to handle the surge of the customers which can cause overcrowding
  • Unpredictable wait times - lack of information about the wait times can lead to customers feeling frustrated and can waste their time waiting for too long on-site

4. User Research:

The team conducted interviews with the potential users of the system.

4.1 Research Method

Research Questionnaire - conducted one-on-one interviews to gather information about the users.

  • What are your challenges with queueing?
  • How do you deal with long queues?
  • How does the current queuing system work?
  • What features do you think that could help improve the system and user experience?

4.3 Insights

After conducting the interview we have gathered the following insights:

  • The common challenge that users face while queuing is the long wait for their turn.
  • If the queue is too long and have wasted so much of their time already, most of them deal with it by leaving the area.
  • The queuing system currently uses physical papers and manually calling for the next customers.
  • For the staff's side they would prefer an automated queuing system where they don’t have to manually call the customers and provide physical paper.
  • For the users, they prefer a system where they could leave the area while waiting for their turn and would be notified when it’s almost their turn.

5. Ideation and Brainstorming:

5.1 Brainstorming Map

The group used a brainstorming map to identify the first SDG they would focus on. After considering different SDGs with their proposed idea for the SDG, the group decided to focus on Goal 9, Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure for the queueing system. The brainstorming map helped the group generate, organize and evaluate the ideas to guide the group the SDG and case study they want to focus on.

5.2 Fishbone Diagram

Fishbone diagram was used to identify the and address the problems within the queuing system of the establishment. The fishbone helped us identify different causes of the main problem which is the inefficient queueing system. With this diagram we were able to visualize the connections between the main problem and the factors causing it.

5.3 SCAMPER

SCAMPER map was used to create possible features that would help create a solution. We were able to think and explore different possible features. The group decided to replace pen and paper queuing tickets with automated tickets that they could view from their phone. With this map we were able to come up with ideas that we have not considered previously.

6.2 Storyboard Narrative

Storyboard: Engr. Herold Bryne Jobs

1. Landing Page Screen

  • Herold goes to the website using his browser. He sees the application’s name, the information about the system, and the benefits of using it. He is also able to see the options to be able to search for any establishment or track the queueing status by entering a valid tracking ID. He also sees the ampplication’s logo at the top navigation bar along with the options “Home”, “About Us”, “Track by ID”, and “Register”. He also sees all the different services he can avail if ever he wants to use the system.

2. Apply for Queue Screen

  • Herold gets redirected to the Apply for Queue screen after searching for a company. He sees the company’s logo and the queueing number of the person currently being entertained by the establishment, as well as how many people are currently waiting. He also sees a form to fill up using his email and phone number and clicks a dropdown to choose his purpose for queueing. Upon pressing the confirm button, a modal appears that displays his queue ID.

3. Track Queue Results Screen

  • Herold navigates to the Track Queue Results screen after queuing up or searching for his tracking number. He sees the logo and name of the establishment he is queueing up for and beside it is his queueing number. He also sees the current queue numbers that are being served by each table of the establishment.

Storyboard: Chatur Silencer

1. Landing Page Screen

  • Silencer wants to try and use the system for the company he works at to improve their queueing system and goes to the landing page screen using his web browser. He scrolls down to see the available services and their respective benefits and prices. He also sees a switch where he can choose to either pay yearly or monthly for the service.

2. Register Company Screen

  • After choosing a service, Silencer is navigated to the Register Company screen. He sees fields where he can fill up his company name, email, contact number, their company’s domain name, and verify the plan selected. He also sees a button to upload their company logo and the register button

3. Payment Link Screen

  • After submitting his application, he is redirected to the Payment Link screen which shows a countdown that would redirect him to the maya payment site. If ever the page does not redirect once the countdown has ended, he will see a clickable link that redirects him to the payment site.

4. Login Screen

  • After receiving access to the system, Silencer goes to the Login screen and attempts to log in to his account with his username and password and presses the login button after inputting into the text fields. He also sees the option to create a new account if ever he does not have one yet.

5. Register User Screen

  • Silencer navigates to the Register User screen to create his very own account. He fills in the required fields of “Email”, “First Name”, “Last Name”, “Username”, and “Password”, He also chooses a role for his account where he picked one of the options from the dropdown menu and then sees a button to register that account into the system.

6. Dashboard Screen

  • Silencer sees the current status of the queueing such as the current queue number he is serving and the total number of people waiting. He also sees the options to add more people to the queue and to call the next person to his teller. When Silencer adds a new queue, he sees a modal that popped where he needs to input the email, phone number, and the purpose of the person queuing up. When Silencer calls the next person to his table, he hears a sound that would trigger, notifying the next person to go to his teller.

7. View Queue Screen

  • Silencer wants to view all the tellers and the respective queue numbers they are currently serving so he can use this screen to be displayed to the public outside waiting in line. So he goes to the View Queue Screen which he accessed after navigating the hamburger menu. Here, he sees all the tellers in the establishment and the respective queue numbers that they are currently serving. He can also see the current date and time of the day.

8. View Records Screen

  • Silencer wants to see the queueing records they have made for the day and goes to the View Records creen which he accessed after navigating the hamburger menu. He sees a table that contains the date of each queue with the necessary information such as the ID, priority number, and timestamp of the queue on when they were created and last updated.

9. User Profile Screen

  • Silencer goes to the User Profile screen to view his information and wants to change his password. After clicking on the button to change his password, a modal will appear where he can fill up his old password, and new password, and confirm his new password.

Storyboard: Administrator

1. Administrator Login Screen

  • The administrator wants to log in to his account and goes to the login screen using his browser. He inputs his username and password and presses the login button after entering his credentials.

2. Manage Affiliated Companies Screen

  • After logging in, the admin is brought to the dashboard of the system which is the Manage Affiliated Companies screen. He sees all affiliated companies in a table with their respective information such as their ID, company name, email, contact number. He also sees the status of each company whether they are active or inactive and the service they availed. He can click on these status buttons to set the status of a certain company.

3. Company Details Screen

  • The admin wants to view the list of user accounts in a particular company. He clicks the row of the company in the dashboard and is redirected to the Company Details screen which contains a display of company details and the table of the list of users in that particular company. It contains the column names “ID”, “Username”, “Name”, and “Role”.

4. Requests Screen

  • Admin wants to check any new companies or establishments who applied to use the system and goes to the request screen. He sees a table that contains all companies that have applied to use the system along with their ID, company name, email, contact number, the service they availed, and the status of their application if they are pending or paid. After confirming the company’s payment, the admin creates the domain for the company that applied. Once finished, the administrator will click the Paid status button to open the Enter Domain Link modal and inputs the designated domain link for the company. This sends an email to the company that their application is successful and sends them the link to their website with the working system.
